    Career

    Tom Hiddleston started his career as a theater actor in 1999. His first role was as Captain Stanhope in the theater production Journey’s End.

    While performing student plays, he began emerging in television, landing parts in Stephen Whittaker’s adaptation of Nicholas Nickleby (2001) for ITV, Conspiracy (2001), and the BBC/HBO drama The Gathering Storm (2002).

    In 2006, Hiddleston made his big screen debut playing the character of Oakley in Joanna Hogg‘s first feature film, Unrelated.  In 2011, Tom’s career took a major twist when he played Loki in the Marvel Studios film, Thor for which he won the Empire Award for Best Male Newcomer in 2012.

    Tom Hiddleston was born as Thomas William Hiddleston on February 9, 1981, in Westminster, London, England, UK. He is of British nationality and is of Mixed ethnicity (English, Welsh, German, and Scottish).

    His father’s name is James Norman Hiddleston and mother is Diana Patricia Hiddleston. His mother is an arts administrator and former stage manager and father a physical chemist.
