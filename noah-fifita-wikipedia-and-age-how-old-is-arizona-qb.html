    How Old Is Arizona QB?

    Noah Fifita Wikipedia: Noah Fifita, the rising star of college football, is making waves as the Arizona Wildcats’ quarterback.

    Fifita ranks as one of his team’s top players and has gained a reputation for his exciting performance in America’s toughest high school league.

    The young adult Fifita is the 10th quarterback in the past 20 seasons to make his first two career starts against AP top 10 opponents.

    Arizona quarterback wears jersey number 11 and was a three-star recruit coming out of high school.

    Quarterback Fifita attended Servite High School in California and honed his football skills there.

    During high school, he teamed up with several other Arizona signees, including wide receiver Tetairoa McMillan, tight end Keyan Burnett, and linebacker Jacob Manu.

    Additionally, Noah took part in the 2022 Polynesian Bowl and earned several accolades, including the titles of CalHi State Sophomore and State Junior of the Year.

    Moreover, he held numerous records at Servite, like career passing touchdowns, yards, and completions.

    For his collegiate career, he committed to play for the Arizona Wildcats and made his debut against the Washington Huskies in October 2023.

    Noah Fifita Wikipedia And Age

    Noah Malakai Fifita was born to father, Les Fifita, and mother, Winnona Fifita, on July 28, 2003, in Huntington Beach, California, United States.

    His parents come from a mixed background, with his father hailing from Tonga and his mother coming from the Philippines.

    Moreover, Noah flaunts the flags of Tonga and the Philippines on Noah’s rib cage pads.

    The 20-year-old athlete takes pride in his roots and emphasizes that his family comes first.

    Noah Family: Family And Siblings

    Noah Fifita is one of the two kids of his parents, Les and Winnona. The 5’11’ college athlete grew up with a sibling, a younger brother named Dash Fifita.

    The Arizona Wildcats quarterback’s success closely ties to the encouragement and guidance of his parents, along with his hard work and talent.

    Besides being a father, Les served as the coach to his son Noah and played a key role in nurturing his football skills.

    Les was the one who recognized Noah’s ability as a quarterback and made the decision to transition him into a quarterback.

    Now, he also coaches other students to prepare them, just as Noah was prepared.

    Father Les wrote on social media, “My whole life has revolved around preparing my sons for every level of football they’ve encountered.”

    Further, he continued, “In the process, God has blessed me with hundreds of other boys who I now consider sons.”

    In contrast, his mother, Winnona, a homemaker, provides Noah with mental support.
