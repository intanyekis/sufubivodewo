    Jamie-Lynn Spears has been openly sparring with Britney Spears on social media and the two are clearly embattled amid the controversial state of their family's affairs. Britney Spears' sister is getting ready to launch her memoir titled Things I Should Have Said, but that's coming with a whole lot of controversy, and it seems that cancel culture is leading the way.

    They simply don't want to cash in on any of the proceeds of the book, as they don't want to be linked to Jamie-Lynn Spears in any official capacity.

    Jamie Lynn Spears Is Very, Very Canceled

    Jamie Lynn Spears has always lived in her sister's shadow. In spite of the fact that she has crafted her own career, she has never been able to produce talent as raw and recognized as Britney Spears, who was quickly able to soar to fame and earn a multimillion fortune.

    In an effort to stay current, she tried to intrigue fans with promise of the 'inside scoop' about her journey through life, which will of course, inevitably feature Britney Spears. Her team helped her line up a charity that was positioned to receive a portion of proceeds from each sale, and they opted to align themselves with an incredible organization called This Is My Brave. This organization is dedicating to giving people a forum to discuss how they overcame challenges with mental illness and addiction.

    Fans, however, are in an uproar over Jamie Lynn's complicit nature throughout the many years that Britney was essentially held in captivity, and they have completely canceled her. Recognizing this tumultuous situation, the charity has pulled out of their agreement and have refused to accept Jamie-Lynn's money.

    The Charity Needs Money, But Doesn't Want Jamie's

    The entire premise behind running a charity is to raise money for the group, and This Is My Brave would love some additional funding — just not from Jamie Lynn.

    Free Britney fans have been rallying against Jamie Lynn, stating that she was complicit in spite of being aware that her sister was being abused and taken advantage of. They're livid that Jamie Lynn seems to be leveraging success from Britney's pain, and they want absolutely nothing to do with her.

    Opting to stay away from dirty money and bad intentions, the charity has decided that any funding coming from anything even remotely connected to Jamie Lynn could potentially be harmful to them, and the cons outweighed the pros in this situation.

    In spite of being aligned as the beneficiary of proceeds from Jamie Lynn's book sale, This Is My Brave has removed themselves and have politely declined the offer of funding.
